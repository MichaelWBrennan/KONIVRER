Pre-History
Summary
• Before stories learned names, debts learned shapes.
• People came late; laws were waiting politely.
• The first sin was waste; the first virtue was shared fire.

The Age Before Grain
Rivers walked; mountains slept. People asked permission to be born and were told to wait. Some did. Their patience became the valley’s stubborn kindness.

The First Bargain
A wolf returned a child and was paid with a name that made it sleep near the door. The child grew to speak well with strangers.

Why the Veilstone Was Made
So that grief would not drown joy by staying. So that joy would not shame grief by leaving too quickly.
